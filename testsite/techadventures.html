    Our Mission

    Tech Adventures strives to relate what you learn in your day to day curriculum to every-day engineering situations that you may encounter in the field.

    • About Us
    • History
    • Achievements

    Tech Adventures was created to bridge the gap between what you learn in school and what experience is expected in the workplace. Our Instructors and Volunteers are all very well versed in their majors and have all applied their knowledge in the field through internship programs. Over the past summer, the Tech Adventures Team has been working on a curriculum as well as securing funding and a location for these classes. Every week there will be a different hands-on project focusing on a different field of engineering. Projects range anywhere from web design to 3D printing. A list of projects and dates can be found under the schedule tab.

    Tech Adventures was the idea of the 2015 - 2016 IEEE Florida Tech Student Branch President, Briana Cantos. A few workshops were held throughout the Spring semester however the program did not officially enter the planning and execution phases until the Summer of 2016 under the current student branch president, Christopher Wille. With help from Briana and other IEEE members John O'Malley and Gio Torres. The first official Tech Adventures workshop will take place in the first few weeks of the semester. The schedule page will be updated when details are finalized.

    Tech Adventures is a very new program that is still under development. We have many goals set for the 2016 - 2017 school year. Check back here in the future to see if we accomplish any!
